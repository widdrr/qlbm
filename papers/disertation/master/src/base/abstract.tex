Although Quantum Computing can potentially contribute to the field of Computational Fluid Dynamics, there are fundamental challenges that prevent simulating complex scenarios on near-term hardware. 
In this thesis, we present, validate and benchmark a fully generic and parameterized simulator based on the Quantum Lattice Boltzmann Method for the Advection-Diffusion equation.
We introduce a method for designing local, linear and left-stochastic boundary conditions for each lattice node inside the quantum circuit.  
The simulator is validated against classical simulations in various scenarios including different dimensionalities, time-varying advection fields and custom boundary conditions.
Both functional and performance limitations of the algorithm are analyzed, with supporting numerical experiments and complexity analysis. 
Ultimately, we show that while achieving numerical accuracy, our method for generalized boundary conditions imposes a significant performance penalty.
